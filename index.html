Sarajevo — Day by Day
Wednesday
Arrival & Old Town
10:00
🕌 Sebilj & Baščaršija
The Ottoman heart of Sarajevo. The 18th-century wooden fountain is the city's most iconic symbol. Legend says if you drink from it, you'll always return. Wander the cobblestone alleys of the old bazaar and its small craft workshops.
11:00
🏛️ Tašlihan Caravanserai
Ruins of a grand Silk Road inn built in 1543 by Gazi Husrev-Bey. Walk the glass walkway above the ancient foundations and imagine centuries of traders and travellers passing through.
13:30
🔥 Vječna vatra — Eternal Flame
A flame burning since 1946 in memory of WWII victims. It was never extinguished even through the devastating 1992–1996 siege. A deeply moving symbol of the city's extraordinary resilience.
14:00
🌇 Latin Bridge — WWI Assassination Site
The exact spot where Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated on 28 June 1914, triggering World War I and the deaths of 16 million people. One of the most historically significant street corners in the world.
💡 Small museum at the north end of the bridge tells the full story
15:00
🕌 Gazi Husrev-beg Mosque
The largest and most beautiful mosque in Bosnia, built in 1532. Free entry outside prayer times. The courtyard with its ornate fountain is an oasis of calm in the heart of the old town.
💡 Cover shoulders and knees. Remove shoes at the entrance.
16:00
🏛️ Vijećnica — City Hall
The spectacular Austro-Hungarian building on the Miljacka riverbank. Burned to the ground during the 1992 siege, painstakingly rebuilt and reopened in 2014. The restored interior with its stained-glass atrium is breathtaking.
19:00
🌅 Yellow Fortress — Sunset Panorama
A 15-minute uphill walk from Baščaršija through quiet neighbourhood streets. The panoramic view of Sarajevo spread across the valley at sunset is one of the most memorable sights in the Balkans.
💡 Arrive 1 hour before sunset — gets busy but the atmosphere is magical.
Wednesday Walking Route — Old Town → Latin Bridge → Yellow Fortress
Thursday
Siege History
11:00
🏛️ War Childhood Museum
One of the most powerful museums in Europe. The siege of Sarajevo told through 50 objects chosen by people who were children during the conflict — each with a personal testimony. Raw, human, and deeply moving. Allow at least 90 minutes.
💡 Opens 11am. Book at warchildhood.org
15:00
🚇 Tunnel of Salvation
The 800-metre tunnel dug under the airport runway in 1993 that kept the besieged city alive — smuggling food, weapons and people. Now a museum with original footage, personal accounts and artefacts from the siege. Entry ~20 KM.
💡 20-min taxi from centre. Opens 8:30am. Cash only.
17:00
🚡 Trebević Cable Car & Olympic Bobsled Track
Ride the cable car up to 1,163m for panoramic city views, then walk 200m to the abandoned 1984 Winter Olympics bobsled track — now covered in murals and graffiti, reclaimed by the forest. Eerie, beautiful, and completely unlike anywhere else.
Thursday — War Childhood Museum → Tunnel of Salvation → Trebević
🌿

Optional — Nature (Thursday morning or anytime)

Vrelo Bosne (Bosna Spring) — a stunning natural park 30 min from the centre by tram. Crystal-clear springs, swans, and a horse-drawn carriage along a tree-lined path. Free entry.
Skakavac Waterfall — a 75m cascade north of the city. 9km circular trail, ~3–4 hrs. Free entry. Wear good shoes.

Saturday afternoon
Return from Mostar
~15:00
🚗 Return from Mostar
~2.5 hour scenic drive back through the Neretva valley. Arrive in time for a relaxed final evening in Sarajevo.
17:30
🔴 Sarajevo Roses
Scattered across the city's pavements — craters left by mortar shells that killed civilians during the siege, now filled with red resin. A haunting, city-wide memorial you'll notice as you walk the streets.
18:30
🏛️ Museum of Crimes Against Humanity 1992–95
Raw and direct documentation of the siege through photographs, testimonies, and evidence. More confrontational than the War Childhood Museum. Not for everyone, but important and deeply impactful.
💡 Check opening hours in advance

Practical Tips
💵
Cash is essential
Always carry Bosnian Marks (KM). Kravice, Tunnel of Salvation, Počitelj, and many museums are cash only. Get KM at an ATM on arrival.
🕌
Mosques
Cover shoulders and knees. Women cover hair. Remove shoes at the entrance. Free entry outside prayer times at most mosques.
🚡
Trebević cable car
Check current operating hours before going. The Olympic bobsled track is 200m from the top station — surreal abandoned history overgrown with nature.
🏰️
Počitelj climb
Wear comfortable shoes — the cobblestone path is steep and exposed in May sun. Bring water. The views from the top over the Neretva are worth every step.
💧
Kravice waterfall
Entry 20 KM/person. Parking 6 KM/day. Changing rooms on site. Go before 11am to avoid tour bus crowds. Swimming possible May–September.
💰
Museum of Crimes
The War Childhood Museum (Thu) and the Museum of Crimes (Sat) cover the same war very differently. Both are worth seeing — plan for 1.5 hrs each.
